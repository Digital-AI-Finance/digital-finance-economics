# Economics of Digital Finance - Syllabus

## Course Information

- **Course Title**: Economics of Digital Finance
- **Level**: BSc (3rd/4th year)
- **Prerequisites**: Microeconomics, Macroeconomics, Money and Banking
- **Duration**: 8 sessions x 45 minutes
- **Format**: Lecture with economic analysis

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Apply monetary economic theory to analyze digital currencies and CBDCs
2. Evaluate payment systems using two-sided market economics
3. Analyze DeFi protocols using market microstructure theory
4. Assess regulatory frameworks using welfare economics
5. Synthesize multiple economic perspectives on digital finance policy

---

## Detailed Lesson Outline

### L01: Introduction to the Economics of Digital Finance
**Duration**: 45 minutes

**Topics**:
- Economic definition of digital finance
- Historical evolution: commodity money to digital currencies
- The four economic lenses framework
- Why economists should study digital finance
- Economic questions vs. technical questions

**Key Readings**:
- Brunnermeier & Niepelt (2019): "On the Equivalence of Private and Public Money"
- BIS Annual Economic Report (2022), Chapter III

---

### L02: Monetary Economics of Digital Currencies
**Duration**: 45 minutes

**Topics**:
- Functions of money revisited
- Quantity theory and velocity in digital age
- Cryptocurrencies as money: economic assessment
- Stablecoins: design, stability, and risks
- Currency substitution and Gresham's Law

**Key Readings**:
- Yermack (2015): "Is Bitcoin a Real Currency?"
- Gorton & Zhang (2023): "Taming Wildcat Stablecoins"

---

### L03: Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs)
**Duration**: 45 minutes

**Topics**:
- CBDC design choices and tradeoffs
- Monetary policy transmission mechanisms
- Bank disintermediation analysis
- Financial inclusion economics
- International currency competition

**Key Readings**:
- Andolfatto (2021): "Assessing the Impact of CBDC on Private Banks"
- Brunnermeier & Landau (2022): "The Digital Euro"

---

### L04: Payment Systems Economics
**Duration**: 45 minutes

**Topics**:
- Network effects in payment systems
- Two-sided market theory and interchange fees
- Cross-border payment inefficiencies
- RTGS vs. deferred net settlement
- Financial inclusion and the unbanked

**Key Readings**:
- Rochet & Tirole (2006): "Two-Sided Markets"
- BIS (2020): "Enhancing Cross-border Payments"

---

### L05: Platform Economics and Token Economics
**Duration**: 45 minutes

**Topics**:
- Platform economics fundamentals
- Network effects and critical mass
- Token economics: utility, governance, security tokens
- Tokenomics design principles
- Winner-take-all dynamics

**Key Readings**:
- Catalini & Gans (2020): "Some Simple Economics of the Blockchain"
- Cong et al. (2021): "Tokenomics"

---

### L06: Market Microstructure in Digital Finance
**Duration**: 45 minutes

**Topics**:
- Market microstructure fundamentals
- Order books vs. AMMs
- Liquidity provision economics
- Price discovery in fragmented markets
- MEV and market quality

**Key Readings**:
- Capponi & Jia (2021): "The Adoption of Blockchain-based Decentralized Exchanges"
- Park (2021): "The Conceptual Flaws of DEX AMMs"

---

### L07: Regulatory Economics of Digital Finance
**Duration**: 45 minutes

**Topics**:
- Economic rationale for financial regulation
- Principles-based vs. rules-based regulation
- Competition policy in digital finance
- Consumer protection economics
- Regulatory arbitrage

**Key Readings**:
- Zetzsche et al. (2020): "Regulating a Revolution"
- FSB (2022): "Assessment of Risks to Financial Stability from Crypto-assets"

---

### L08: Synthesis - The Future of Digital Finance
**Duration**: 45 minutes

**Topics**:
- Systemic risk in digital finance
- TradFi-DeFi convergence scenarios
- Open research questions
- Policy recommendations synthesis
- Four lenses integration

**Key Readings**:
- Aramonte et al. (2021): "DeFi Risks and the Decentralization Illusion"
- IMF (2023): "Global Financial Stability Report - Crypto Chapter"
